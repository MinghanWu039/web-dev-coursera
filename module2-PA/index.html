Three texts

Part 1
Reading, writing, speaking, and listening were cornerstones of Western rhetorical studies for more than 2,000 years.
Part 2
But in the 20th century recovery of rhetoric within composition studies, reading and writing reign as the dominant tropes for interpretive invention; speaking places a respectable third; listening runs a poor, poor fourth.
Part 3
Indeed, listening has been neglected. That's not to say listening has never been mentioned within 20th-century scholarship. It has. For example, James Phelan and Andrea Lunsford employ listening to explore voices speaking or not speaking within written texts.
